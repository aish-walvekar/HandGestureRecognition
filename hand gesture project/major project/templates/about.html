The primary purpose of "HandyMath: A Gesture Controlled Calculator" is to provide a tool that fosters engagement and learning in mathematics while addressing the needs of users with physical limitations. By offering an alternative input method based on hand gestures, HandyMath opens up new possibilities for individuals who may struggle with conventional input devices due to disabilities or other challenges. HandyMath is an innovative calculator application that utilizes gesture recognition technology to allow users to perform mathematical calculations using hand movements. Developed with a focus on inclusivity, HandyMath provides an intuitive and interactive interface that caters to both children and individuals with physical challenges. Using a webcam or camera-equipped device, HandyMath detects hand gestures and translates them into numerical inputs and arithmetic operations. Users can simply make gestures in the air to input numbers, select operators, and perform calculations without the need for traditional keyboard or touchscreen input. The application offers real-time text and voice feedback, ensuring users can verify their inputs and results effortlessly. By leveraging modern web technologies and prioritizing accessibility, HandyMath aims to make math learning enjoyable and accessible for everyone, fostering an inclusive environment for mathematical exploration and skill development.
